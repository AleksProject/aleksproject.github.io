The
Desert

Deserts are dry, barren, hot areas of land that makes living conditions harsh for organisms that live in the desert. The average climate of the desert is around 60-70° F. Deserts are located in between 30°N and 30°S, for example, you can find deserts in America, Mexico, Arabian Pennisula, North Africa, Australia , Mongolia, Etc. The desert recieves very little rain since deserts are close to mountains so rain clouds can't get in to the region. Due to this low amount of precipitation, the desert only get around 25 cm of rain a year, and low biodiversity.

Unique features
of the Desert

Some features deserts have that others biomes don't include dry air, large bodies of sand, and low supply of water. Deserts are important since the sry conditition helps the concentration of minerals. Due to the high source or minerals, humans use chemicals to mine in the desert, killing plants and animals. '

The Plants
of the Desert

Some plants found in the desert are The Peyote Cactus, The Mexican Poppy, and the Desert Christmas Tree. The Peyote cactus has hallucigenic effects to keep animals from eating it and it can store water. The Mexican Poppy can survive during a drought and survive with poor soil. The Poppy also has toxic latex that can poison animals. Finally the Desert Christmas Tree because it has long stems that can soak up a lot of water.

The Animals
of the Desert

Some of the Animals found in the desert are gray foxes, camels, and burro. The gray fox can dig holes to find areas to store food and find water. Camels have large humps in their back that contain water so they stay cool in the heat. Finally the burros can tolerate a water loss as high as 30% of their body weight.
